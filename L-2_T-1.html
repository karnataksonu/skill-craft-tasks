Who was Mother Teresa?

Mother Teresa, born Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu, was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary known for her selfless service to the poor and sick. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, an organization dedicated to helping the "poorest of the poor." She worked tirelessly in the slums of Kolkata (Calcutta), India, bringing care, dignity, and love to those in need.

Admirable Qualities

  • Compassion: She devoted her life to serving the marginalized and abandoned.
  • Humility: Despite global fame, she lived a simple and humble life.
  • Faith: Her deep spiritual strength fueled her mission.
  • Dedication: She never wavered in her lifelong commitment to humanity.

Legacy

Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979, Mother Teresa became a symbol of peace and kindness across the globe. She was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta in 2016. Her legacy continues to inspire millions to serve with love and compassion.
